## Gap 1, WHAT THEY ACTUALLY WANT (the "feel accomplished")
The business equivalent of the Peloton accomplishment badge is NOT a better P&L or a mastered funnel. It's a delivered sense of "handled" made of four felt states, none requiring expertise:
- **Marcus (drowning) = the dread removed.** "I don't want a report. I want to not lie awake wondering what I'm forgetting." Win-feeling: *I'm not drowning.*
- **Diane + Erin (owner + office manager) = looking competent to the people who matter** (doctor, spouse, staff). Erin: "I want the doctor to think I've got it under control... If a tool makes me look on top of it, I'll defend it." (Corroborates the operator's real optimization: not-getting-blamed + looking-good, not performance.)
- **Tony (non-dental) = quiet reliability in his own words.** "I don't want a dashboard, I want my chairs full and my phone answered." Win-feeling: *my place runs itself.*
- **Priya (numbers) = trustworthy-without-checking, but I could check.** "Verdict up front, but let me click one level down and see the records. The day I catch one wrong number, I'm gone." Win-feeling: *I can stop double-checking because it's correct.*
- **Ray (burned) = it did what it said, on MY business, before I trusted it.**
Peloton delivered "I worked out with a community and feel strong" at 95% retention, no cyclist skill required [VERIFIED: Darden/Extole]. Alloro's equivalent is *"I'm handling my business"* as outcome + feeling, not skill.

## Gap 2, THE ECONOMIC + EMOTIONAL PAIN (the dread is rational)
- **Overhead ~74.62% of collections** (a $1M practice keeps ~$250K, ~25%). [VERIFIED: Dental Economics/Levin]
- **Payroll is the largest fixed cost** (~40-60% of revenue for skilled healthcare). [VENDOR: NetSuite/Rippling] Diane: "Payroll is Friday whether the schedule was full or not. That's the number that wakes me up."
- **Empty chair-hour ~$500-800**; practices cited losing ~$105-240K/yr to no-shows. [VENDOR, directional] Dental visits run ~3x longer than primary care (48.7 vs 17.4 min) [VERIFIED: NIH/PMC]. *Do NOT cite a hard US dental no-show % (the one rigorous figure, 42.68%, is a single Saudi clinic; credible US medical is ~5-7% MGMA).*
- **A missed call = the LTV, not the lead cost:** acquisition ~$150-300, but a new dental patient's LTV ~$5,500-7,500 (~$6,700). [VENDOR] Marcus: "That was six grand and I'll never know it happened."
- **THE IRON-CLAD ONE, cash fragility:** median US small business holds **27 cash-buffer days; 25% hold under 13.** By vertical: auto repair 18, barbers/personal services 21, construction 20, restaurants 16, dental/vet 30. [VERIFIED: JPMorgan Chase Institute, 597k businesses, ~470M transactions] Tony: "Two, three slow weeks and I'm choosing between rent and paying my guys."
- **Dentist personal debt:** 2024/25 grads who borrowed averaged **~$297,800**. [VERIFIED: ADEA] Priya: "Growth isn't aspiration, it's debt service."
- **What "handled" relieves:** the feeling sits on a thin residual + ~20-day buffer + six-grand-per-missed-call + personal debt. The dread is rational. Alloro's job: make the scariest leaks (empty chair, unconverted call, the qualification middle no vendor owns) FEEL closed and actually be closed, without the owner learning how.

## Gap 3, WHAT BURNED THEM (5 named betrayal patterns, real evidence)
1. **The contract trap.** Podium (BBB: "automatically renewed... cancellation was not permitted," charged $998.47 after cancel), Birdeye ("auto renews... never made adequately clear"), Weave ("price lock" that was a multi-year contract; 17 unanswered BBB complaints). [VERIFIED: BBB]
2. **The guaranteed-leads / #1 lie.** FTC order, HomeAdvisor/Angi up to **$7.2M**: sold leads as "consumers who intend to hire soon" that often didn't, "at rates much higher than it can substantiate." [VERIFIED: FTC, the unimpeachable one] "Guaranteed SEO/#1" is a recognized scam (Google warns); agencies hold the GBP hostage. [VERIFIED] Tony: "Guaranteed me #1... for a term nobody searches. Technically kept his word. Totally screwed me."
3. **Vanity metrics.** "Celebrate 50,000 impressions and a 3.2% CTR while owners think 'did we get any customers?'"; reports engineered so it's "harder for clients to question the agency's value." [VERIFIED: industry] = the disconnect brief's core.
4. **The tool that ADDED work (the anti-Peloton).** 70% of software rollouts fail; only 49% of features used; 72% abandon dashboards for spreadsheets (dashboard "sits outside the workflow"). [VERIFIED] Erin: "It cost me time. I quietly stopped using it and told the doctor it didn't work." (The adoption-death the operator-reality brief predicts.)
5. **Surprise fees / enterprise misfit.** Weave setup $500-750 + form-migration fees; HubSpot 5x-20x cost increases + lock-in; Zenoti "far more than you need" for a 1-5 location shop. [VERIFIED] Ray: "Setup fee they didn't mention, price doubles at renewal, built for a 40-chair group and I've got 3 ops."

**The pattern, so Alloro inverts each:** (1) lock-in you can't escape, (2) promises they can't keep, (3) vanity metrics instead of customers, (4) work added not removed, (5) surprise fees + enterprise misfit. Every one is a broken "I'll handle it for you."

## Gap 3b, the #1 complaint the 5 patterns under-weight: POST-SALE ABANDONMENT (Notion Session-Research-Capture migration 2026-07-08)
A late-June competitor review-mining pass (9 categories, 2 red-team passes; aggregator-sourced, G2/Trustpilot/Reddit 403-blocked so labeled) found the **#1 complaint across ALL 9 categories was post-sale abandonment**: "attentive in the sale, crickets after signing." It out-ranks even lock-in and vanity-metrics. Alloro's embedded-accountable-partner model is the direct inversion. Verbatim phrase bank (outreach gold, their words not ours):
- "a direct, traceable line between what I'm paying you and new paying customers who walked through my door"
- "the single most important move to make this month AND confirm my last move actually worked"
- "the phone is ringing, but the appointment book isn't filling up"
- "The product I am receiving does NOT match the product I was sold."
**KEEP/ADD (from real recordings):** customers buy the TEAM / outcomes, not the dashboard (Garrison, the happiest payer, has never opened the product). KEEP = embedded accountable partner + named humans/dollars behind the number. ADD (unanimous) = conversion truth PAST the first touch ("convert past the free consultation... it's almost inflated").

**The "false sense of coverage" trap (cross-vertical VoC, barbershop, from the Objection-Library migration 2026-07-08):** the deepest form of Betrayal 4 (the tool that failed silently). Jack Nolan / Jodi at Ray's Place Barbershop (Bend OR), the booking app went down mid-haircut and the owner didn't even know booking + web were the same vendor. The owner pays an agency, BELIEVES they're handled, and has NO visibility when it breaks. The tell "I already have someone handling that" is a belief system built on sunk cost, NOT a price objection. Emotion: trapped, embarrassed, resigned. Reframe (Alloro's opening): don't compete with the agency, BE the layer that watches whether the agency's work is producing results. **Barbershop = non-dental, so it independently validates the universal-ICP thesis.** (Also: Maria, "for every $10K spent we had less than $1K revenue," 3 failed campaigns; Jodi, "in my 52 years I've learned that if things start bad, they never get better.")

## THE PELOTON PLAY (4 moves, each delivers a felt state + refuses a betrayal)
1. **Deliver the feeling of HANDLED, never the work of handling.** Done-for-you IS the screen-on-the-bike. Design law: does this give the feeling without asking them to learn/work/improve? A feature that makes them a "better operator" FAILS. (Kills Betrayal 4.)
2. **Verdict first, proof underneath.** Front-door the plain "you're okay, here's the one move" (Marcus/Tony/Diane feel handled) with "show me the numbers" one click down (Priya/Ray earn trust). The badge is real because the number is real. (Kills Betrayal 3.)
3. **Report the outcome they asked for, booked/qualified/showed-up, never clicks/impressions.** (Anti-FTC-HomeAdvisor pattern.)
4. **Prove it on THEIR business before the ask; never lock them in.** Month-to-month, no guaranteed-outcome claims (Value #6), because the audience is scar-tissue + ~20-day cash-thin. (Kills Betrayals 1, 2, 5.)

**Win the front desk (Erin) or the purchase dies.** The doctor buys; the untrained multi-hat operator decides if it lives. Point the "screen on the bike" at her too: make her day easier + make her look competent to the doctor. That's the retention loop.

**The community element (honest):** Alloro's analog is NOT a social feed of dentists (no time, won't perform, Marcus/Tony bounce). It's:
- **The instructor -> the delivered verdict as a trusted voice** ("you're okay, here's the one thing"). The strongest analog.
- **The badge -> a felt "you're handling it" signal** on real numbers (full week, recovered patient, leak closed).
- **The leaderboard -> "how am I doing vs practices like mine," only if verifiable** (Priya's veto).
- **[INFERRED, flagged]** a genuine peer community is a WEAKER fit than for Peloton. The retention engine is the instructor-verdict relationship + the accomplishment feeling, not a network. THIS is the single assumption most worth testing.

## What it means (built / positioned / adopted)
- **Built:** done-for-you outcome engine + verdict-with-proof, pointed at the leak-in-the-middle. No dashboards-to-operate. Accuracy is the ship-blocker (one wrong number = churn).
- **Positioned:** "the Peloton of local-business growth." Sell the FEELING of handled, never mastery/"become a better entrepreneur." Sell "booked, qualified, showed-up," never leads/clicks. No guarantees.
- **Adopted:** win the front desk by removing her work + making her look good; prove on real numbers before the ask; month-to-month, no lock-in.
