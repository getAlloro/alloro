# Practice-Side vs. Vendor-Side Disconnect in Dental (research brief)

*Stored 2026-07-07. External, non-circular sources (consultant/practice-management literature, not Alloro's own customers). Grading: [VERIFIED] = quoted from a named cited source; [INFERRED] = synthesis across sources. Commissioned by Corey to answer: how do PRACTICES (and the people in them) talk about operating, vs how MARKETING/vendors talk, and where is the disconnect (the minivan-vs-convertible gap).*

**Standing caveat on numbers:** nearly every conversion statistic below comes from consultants/vendors who sell training/software, and the figures are wildly inconsistent (national new-patient call conversion quoted as 23%, 42%, 50-60%, "top 80-90%"). Treat all conversion percentages as self-interested and directionally useful, NOT independently established. Do not cite a specific number as fact.

## 1. How practices + their people ACTUALLY talk about operating

**The center of gravity is the phone call (a human), not the website.**
- [VERIFIED] "98% of new patients call a dental office before their first visit"; "67% of callers immediately call a competitor if they can't reach your office." (Scheduling Institute)
- [VERIFIED] The ADA's own intake guidance is a human-conversation protocol: answer by the third ring, "a smile in his/her voice," gather the reason for the call (esp. whether it's an emergency), last visit, availability, medical issues, and dental-benefit coverage. (ADA patient intake)

**The qualification step is taught as rapport + discovery, NOT gatekeeping or price-quoting.**
- [VERIFIED] MGE: "Your goal is to get them in the chair! Try to schedule every person who calls." On price shoppers: "you have no idea what this patient needs... only your doctor can determine this." Taught redirect: "do you mind if I ask you a couple of questions first?" / "I'd like to just get you in and have the doctor take a look."
- [VERIFIED] All-Star Dental Academy: "Case acceptance is beginning the moment I start to build rapport and trust." A transactional "we don't work with your insurance, bye" call is how practices destroy "$40,000+ cases."
- [INFERRED] Nuance for Alloro: "qualification" in the practice's vocabulary does NOT mean *filter out the unqualified* (the SaaS/sales meaning). It means *diagnose the human's real want, build trust, and get them in the chair so the doctor can determine the actual treatment.* Disqualifying facts (exact insurance, price) are deliberately DEFERRED. This is the opposite of an auto-responder that answers "do you take my insurance?" and ends the conversation.

**Why practices are wary of pure online self-scheduling / auto-response.**
- [VERIFIED] Patient Prism: online scheduling "might reduce the sense of commitment... Telling someone over the phone creates a verbal commitment that is psychologically more binding than clicking a button." Online systems "may not effectively triage patients based on urgency."
- [VERIFIED] Generic self-scheduling tools "don't sync with dental PMS systems, cannot enforce procedure-specific time blocks, and create double-booking problems."
- [INFERRED] The fear is concrete: a new-patient exam needs the right chair-time length, the right provider, insurance checked, emergencies triaged up. Self-scheduling lets a patient book a 30-min cleaning for what's actually a root-canal emergency, or no-show because they never spoke to a person. Control-of-schedule + correctness, not technophobia.

**The real pain = the intake system, not lead volume.**
- [VERIFIED] "The best marketing campaign in the world cannot overcome a broken intake process." (Scheduling Institute)
- [VERIFIED] MGE: national average call conversion "just 23 percent... more than three out of four potential new patients who call are hanging up without booking." Bottleneck = "front desk phone skills and internal case acceptance systems, not ad campaigns."

## 2. THE DISCONNECT: vendor-sells-X vs practice-actually-wants-Y (minivan vs convertible)

| Vendor sells (X) | Practice actually wants (Y) |
|---|---|
| More leads / clicks / form fills; agencies report clicks not chairs | Booked, qualified patients who show up + accept treatment |
| Online self-scheduling ("book 24/7 without the phone") | The human triage conversation (chair time, insurance, emergency, binding verbal commitment) |
| Missed-call auto-text | A person who answers by the third ring and *converts* the call |
| Reviews / reputation as the growth engine | A fixed intake/front-desk system (reviews don't matter if 3 of 4 callers hang up) |
| Services sold by the metric, paid regardless of results | Accountability for the outcome (a booked patient); someone who listens to the calls |
| Generic templated marketing | Specificity: specific proof/outcomes/reasons to call |
| A self-serve platform the staff must operate | Done-for-you that doesn't ADD work to an overwhelmed front desk |
| Volume of inquiries (Google Ads call volume) | Commitment quality (high-volume ad clicks "shop around and don't commit") |

**One-line gap [INFERRED]:** vendors sell *acquisition + automation the practice must operate*; practices need *conversion + human judgment done for them.* The money is lost between the lead arriving and a qualified patient being booked, and NO vendor owns that middle. That middle is the qualification conversation.

## 3. Staff-adoption reality

**Why they resist:**
- [VERIFIED] Emotional, not technical: "People don't resist change because they're lazy. They resist because they're unsure, unincluded, or unprepared." (AADOM/Laura Nelson)
- [VERIFIED] Comfort with the old way + tools that fight the workflow; "endless training that front desk staff dodge can cause dental PMS adoption to stall hard"; under-adoption is the norm.

**What gets them to adopt:**
- [VERIFIED] AADOM 5 steps: explain the WHY, involve staff early, give space to learn, celebrate small wins, keep the dialogue. "Change doesn't work without leadership... bring your team with you."
- [VERIFIED] Winning AI frame: "AI won't replace your team. It will give them time back by automating the repeatable stuff so they can focus on the human side of dentistry."
- [VERIFIED] Hands-on pre-launch training flips skeptics into advocates within weeks.
- [INFERRED] The office manager is the gatekeeper + the doctor's proxy for operational reality. A tool that threatens the front desk (adds work, replaces judgment, implies they're the problem) gets quietly non-adopted, and the doctor sees "it didn't work," not "we didn't adopt it." Adoption is won at the front desk, not in the doctor's demo.

## 4. Implications for Alloro
1. **Own the missing MIDDLE, not the ends.** Every vendor sells the top (leads/reviews/website) or the tail (self-scheduling). The money leaks in the *qualification conversation* between them. Alloro fits by improving/doing THAT (human-in-the-loop handling of a new inquiry), not another acquisition channel or auto-responder.
2. **Position as "qualified, booked patient," not "leads."** The practice's success vocabulary is "showed up, scheduled a second appt, accepted treatment." Disclaim the lead-volume frame they've been burned by.
3. **Do NOT lead with self-scheduling or full auto-response for new patients.** On-canon design = assist/prepare the human (qualify the inquiry, verify insurance, hand the front desk a ready-to-book, triaged patient), NOT replace the human's booking decision.
4. **Respect "qualification = diagnose the want, don't filter."** An over-eager gate that lets a caller disqualify themselves (price/insurance) is the mistake consultants train against. Build to build rapport + surface the real want + move toward the chair, deferring disqualifying detail.
5. **Win the front desk, or the doctor's purchase dies.** Design + pitch so the front desk is relieved of repeatable work and made to look good, never replaced or blamed. Done-for-you is an adoption advantage (removes the training the front desk dodges).
6. **Accountability for outcomes is a wedge.** Report the booked-qualified-patient outcome (not clicks); directly attacks the top grievance (agencies paid regardless of results).

**Generalizes [INFERRED]** to any high-consideration local-service business where the job needs human triage and staff is the adoption gatekeeper (med spa, vet, HVAC/home services, law, aesthetic health). Same shape: vendors sell leads/reviews/booking-automation; money leaks in the qualification conversation; staff distrusts tools that add work or replace judgment.
