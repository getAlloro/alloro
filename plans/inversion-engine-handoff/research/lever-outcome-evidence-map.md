# Lever -> outcome evidence map (for the honest simulation)

Graded evidence for whether each dashboard lever CAUSALLY affects local-business outcomes. Researched 2026-07-07 (verify-don't-assert, after Claude over-asserted "no causal data exists"; Corey pushed back, correctly).

**THE LOAD-BEARING HONESTY FRAME:** almost every effect below is a POPULATION AVERAGE / CORRELATION, mostly measured on E-COMMERCE (not local-service bookings), with selection bias. They are legit PRIORS/RANGES to seed a simulation, LABELED "estimate, not a promise" (Value #6), NEVER guarantees. The per-business CAUSAL number can only come from Alloro's OWN first-party measured funnel, which the attribution rail (Ch5b: become-the-responder + capture visitor->booking) generates. That capture converts borrowed benchmarks into an owned, honest, per-practice causal model (the moat + the Kenji optimization loop).

## STRATEGIC BOUNDARY (Corey 2026-07-07): a bounded, WINNABLE game
**E-commerce has NO Google Business Profile.** By staying in the LOCAL-SERVICE lane (GBP-anchored, a physical service area), Alloro stays OUT of the entire broad e-commerce / SEO war. The customer's competitive set is NOT the internet, it is the ~10-20 similar businesses in their SERVICE AREA. That is the concrete form of "un-comparable / finite levers / not rocket science": the game is BOUNDED and LOCAL, and Alloro's only job is to make its customer win against a handful of nearby competitors, a moat by SCOPE. Implications for this map:
- The GBP + local-pack + local-search levers below ARE the real game and are already LOCAL-sourced (Whitespark, Google GBP, Sterling Sky), correct for us, not borrowed.
- The two e-commerce-derived CONVERSION priors (reviews->conversion Spiegel; site-speed Google/Deloitte) are BORROWED from an adjacent lane, directional only; validate with Alloro's OWN local booking data via the attribution rail. A high-consideration LOCAL booking (choosing a dentist) likely weights trust-signals like reviews MORE than an e-commerce click, not less.
- Keep distinct: the ~10-20 bound is the LOCAL-PACK / local-search game; the AEO / AI-answer front ("best dentist near me" in ChatGPT/Gemini) is a newer, less-bounded competitive front.

## Strongest (seed as labeled priors)
- **Speed-to-lead:** 5-min response ~21x more likely to qualify, ~100x to connect (MIT/InsideSales 2007, ~15k leads); within 1hr ~7x (HBR 2011, 2,241 firms). Two independent large observational studies; the library's designated verified lever. [study, observational]
- **Reviews -> conversion:** showing reviews ~+270% (up to 380% high-consideration), Spiegel/Northwestern; rating PEAKS at 4.2-4.5, declines toward 5.0 (too-perfect = distrust). [study, e-commerce]
- **Site speed:** +0.1s ~+8.4% retail conversion (Google/Deloitte 2020); ~-4.4% conversion per added second 0-5s (Portent). [study, e-commerce]

## Middle (directional benchmarks only)
- **GBP photos:** +42% direction requests, +35% website clicks (Google first-party, correlational, method undisclosed). [benchmark]
- **Review reply-rate:** 88% would use a business that replies to all vs 47% none (BrightLocal 2024; STATED intent, not measured behavior). [benchmark]
- **4-star threshold:** 40% won't consider a business under 4 stars (BrightLocal). [benchmark]

## Weakest / DO NOT dress up (honesty guardrails for the build)
- **GBP posts -> rank = measured NULL** (Sterling Sky test): posts CONVERT, they do NOT rank. The engine must NEVER imply posting improves rank. (Internal library already flagged the audit's "gbp_readiness implies rank impact" as dishonest, agent-a06519a86c05.)
- **NAP consistency:** the "+16% / 23% / 11%-weight" numbers are untraceable blog lore; Google (Mueller) normalizes minor variations. Weakest quantified lever.
- **SEO / content output:** "+50-140% traffic in 90 days" = vendor lore (AutoSEO anti-pattern).
- **Maps-rank -> revenue (causally):** proximity confound; the "126% more traffic" is unverified blog lore.

## Bottom line
An honest research-grounded simulation is PARTIAL now: ~3-4 levers seedable with defensible labeled priors (speed-to-lead, reviews->conversion, site-speed). The rest is expert opinion, vendor lore, or a null result, and cannot be simulated honestly without Alloro's own measured before/after data. The per-practice causal model is DOWNSTREAM of the attribution rail (Ch5b).
