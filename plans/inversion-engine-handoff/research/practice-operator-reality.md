# Practice operator reality (Corey primary-source, 2026-07-07)

The layer UNDER the vendor-disconnect (`practice-operations-vs-vendor-disconnect.md`): WHO runs a local-service practice and what they are actually optimizing for. Corey's lived experience (ortho); primary-source, stronger than the secondhand consultant blogs. Should inform VoC/ICP canon (Corey stakes) and ground the next research pass.

## The reality
- Vendors build B2B for ESTABLISHED companies with traditional roles, org charts, and trained-role owners. Local-service practices do NOT operate that way. This mismatch is why vendors keep selling the convertible to the minivan mom.
- Most often there is ONE owner who works IN the business, and the business depends on that person (e.g., the doctor).
- They are not seasoned entrepreneurs, so they do not know, cannot afford, or choose not to invest in strong hires. The doctor becomes the accidental CEO (never owned or run a business) AND, by default, head of marketing, HR, finance, and everything else.
- They hire a practice manager to help, who is then handed titles they were never trained for. Corey: he has never seen a "head of marketing" in an ortho practice who was an actual trained, experienced marketer. It is always a second hat.

## The consequence (the operating truth)
- People learn to do things the way they CAN, not the way they SHOULD.
- They are not incentivized by improved performance, so keeping life EASY and FAMILIAR beats better-but-harder, every time.
- So the real operator optimizes for: not getting blamed, looking good to the doctor, a calmer/easier day, job security. NOT a KPI. (My inference, flagged; a target for the deepening pass.)

## The competitive advantage for Alloro
- Build for the REAL operator (untrained, multi-hat, optimizing for easy/familiar), NOT the trained-role professional the vendors imagine.
- Done-for-you fits this exactly: it removes the need for the untrained person to do the thing right. The pitch that lands is "makes your day easier and makes you look good," not "improves performance."

## The Peloton frame (Corey, 2026-07-07): deliver the FEELING, not the mastery
The owner does NOT want to invest time/money to learn new systems, adopt new techniques, or BECOME a better entrepreneur. Analogy: most Peloton riders are not trying to improve their cycling form or become better cyclists, they are not looking at FTP or LTRs (the serious-cyclist metrics), they just want to FEEL ACCOMPLISHED. Peloton won by putting a screen on a bike and bringing the workout community to the busy mom, meeting her where she is, not making her a better cyclist.

**Alloro = the Peloton of local-business growth:** give the owner the FEELING (clarity, confidence, "I'm handling my business," accomplished, not drowning) WITHOUT the work or learning. Do NOT sell self-improvement, mastery, or "become a better entrepreneur." Meet them where they are. Done-for-you IS the screen-on-the-bike: it delivers the outcome and the feeling without requiring them to become the expert.

**The design test this creates:** every product + positioning decision is judged by "does this give them the feeling of handled/accomplished without asking them to learn, work, or improve?", NOT "does this make them a better operator?" A feature that requires the owner to get better at their business fails this test even if it "works."

## Two surfaces + the Peloton-not-Zwift lesson (Corey, 2026-07-07)
**Alloro Connect ("Connect with your community") = done-FOR-you.** The screen-on-the-bike: Alloro does the work and brings the owner their community + outcome. The household-name mass play (reconcile with memory `project_alloro_connect`).
