## The winners' common pathway (PROVEN across all)
1. **ONE concrete, self-proving outcome — never "a platform."** A skeptical owner said yes in one sentence.
2. **Proof the owner SEES fast, in their own numbers** (review count in week one; +$10k/mo; caller greeted by name day one).
3. **Narrow ICP / one vertical, go deep, refuse off-ICP revenue** (Owner tightened ICP to fix churn; ServiceTitan "blinders on").
4. **The wedge installs a channel/rail later products ride at ~0 marginal CAC** (the phone, the SMS line, the review pipe).
5. **Repeatable acquisition, then the flywheel** — inbound content, inside-sales, or association channels; the flywheel turns when word-of-mouth + expansion outrun churn + CAC.

## The 2-3 moves that separated scalers from stall-outs
1. **Productize** so revenue decouples from headcount (a software/data layer on the DFY work).
2. **ICP discipline** — dominate one vertical, say no (Owner rejects ~40% of prospects; ServiceTitan refused off-ICP).
3. **Prove attributable ROI so retention holds** — self-evident in a dashboard, not a claim.

## The anti-pattern (why most DFY SMB plays stay small forever)
A **custom service** (labor scales linearly, capped by the founder) delivering an **unprovable outcome**
(leads/traffic the owner can't attribute) to **any client who'll pay** (no ICP → churn + thrash).
- **~80% of marketing agencies never break $5M ARR** [widely cited but blog-sourced (learntoscale.us; medium), no primary study; treat as directional folk-stat, not a hard fact].
- Agency net margin *falls* with scale: 19% (studio) → 8% (large) [Promethean Research]. Revenue tracks headcount ~1:1 (~$172k/employee) [AgencyCPAs].
- Services trade at ~1-3x revenue (WPP/Omnicom 0.7-1.4x) vs SaaS 6-15x; services gross margin 30-50% vs SaaS 60-90%+ [Damodaran; a16z].
- **The escape:** productize (fixed scope, published price, "you just do the thing"), narrow the ICP, make ROI self-evident. Narrowed/productized firms hit ~30% margins (2x the 13% avg) [Promethean].
- Failure set: ReachLocal ($514M rev 2013 → sold ~$156M), Hibu (bankrupt), Yodle/Vivial/Web.com/Demandforce/Signpost (shrank/exited cheap) — all sold **unprovable, commoditized lead-gen**.

## The proof → testimonial → referral flywheel (mechanics, PROVEN)
The flywheel turns on **manufacturing an undeniable, attributable result the customer observes in their
OWN data** — everything downstream (testimonial, referral, cheaper CAC) is a byproduct. Scalers put
~80% of the engineering into this Stage 1, not the "ask."
1. **Manufacture** an undeniable, attributable result (natively yours because you're the system of record for it).
2. **Capture** it at the moment of the win, on a recurring cadence (Owner's monthly progress calls) — a byproduct of service, not a special ask.
3. **Weaponize** proof into acquisition (social proof raises purchase likelihood up to 270% [Northwestern Spiegel]; ratings convert best at 4.2-4.5, *not* a perfect 5.0 — buyers discount too-perfect proof).
4. **Referral: the result itself does the soliciting.** Dominant driver was the result + peer community, NOT cash incentive. Rewards *reduce* recommendation trust for innovative offerings [JAMS 2019; PMC] — so **earned, never solicited** is not a handicap, it's the evidence-backed move.
5. **Compound** — retention + a widening proof library make each turn cheaper (ServiceTitan <1%/mo churn; a $70k reference won its largest deal 6 years later).

## Closest analog to Alloro's lane
**PatientPop** — DFY *presence* for local practices (website/SEO/reviews/booking, no PMS core), reached
>$1B. **But it merged with a PMS (Kareo) to get there durably.** No source states presence-alone stalled
(INFERRED), but it's real evidence against "presence-only scales without touching the system-of-record."
**Pressure-test this before it's load-bearing** — it challenges Alloro's payments-not-PMS boundary.
