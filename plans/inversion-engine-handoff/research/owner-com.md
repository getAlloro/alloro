## What it is
Done-for-you growth platform for restaurants (commission-free online ordering, websites, marketing).
Began as a ~$2k/mo SEO/website builder; pivoted (COVID, 2020) to commission-free branded online
ordering — "keep the sales you already make instead of renting them from DoorDash." ~$1M ARR (2020)
→ ~$34M (2024, est.) → ~$1B valuation (2025). *[PROVEN trajectory; exact ARR figures = Sacra/Contrary
estimates, not company-confirmed — verify before quoting a dollar figure.]*

## Adam Guild's stated playbook (his own words, sourced)
1. **Be the whole funnel yourself early** — sales + CS + weekly Loom videos. [First Round podcast — PROVEN]
2. **Won the first customer on pure ROI** — "prove for 90 days you can grow our sales, we'll pay it." [podcast]
3. **Value is ≥50% emotional**, not just utilitarian. [First Round article]
4. **Start with a scrappy FREE MVP on the exact pain.** [Forbes]
5. **Narrow the ICP deliberately** — rejected dog-grooming for restaurants ("wouldn't be able to build an ambitious software company serving dog grooming"). [podcast / Fortune]
6. **Premium price as a forcing function** — $2k/mo, ~20x competitors; "a high price is a great forcing function for an excellent product." [First Round]
7. **Multi-product early lifted results AND retention** — "one of the best decisions we ever made." [First Round]
8. **Recruiting = 30%+ of CEO time**; "results are downstream of team"; hire "forces of nature." [First Round]
9. **Trust earned by aligning to their sales, not extracting** — "I'd earned their trust by making a promise to continue to help them drive their sales." [First Round]
10. **The pivot came from pure listening** — cold-called hundreds of owners: "how can I be helpful to you during this time?" [Forbes]

**His stated sequence for early founders:** talk to customers + take them literally → free scrappy MVP
on their pain → be the whole funnel yourself → prove ROI on performance → *then* charge premium →
go multi-product → pour 30%+ of time into recruiting forces of nature.

## The flywheel (how it scaled)
>70% of customers came **inbound** via a "Moneyball for restaurants" content channel. The result was
**co-observed in the owner's own data** (first customer tracked to "+$10k/month over 90 days," measured
*with* them), which drove organic referrals and fed the content. Owner earns ~$1.50/order, so its own
revenue *is* the customer's result — bigger result → more Owner revenue → better testimonial → cheaper
acquisition. [Contrary Research; Guild, First Round]

## Alloro relevance
Guild's loop — narrow ICP → undeniable ROI first → premium flat price → multi-product — is a near-exact
external validation of Alloro's **$2k flat + verdict-before-pitch** model. **The one divergence to
respect:** Guild leaned on a performance **guarantee** ("prove for 90 days"); Alloro's Value #6 forbids
guarantees → use **Birdeye's guarantee-free self-proving loop** instead (owner watches the number move,
no promise). Everything else transfers.

## Legal caution (from the Notion migration 2026-07-08)
Owner.com was **sued by a rival over a competitor-grading tool** alleged to assign artificially low scores. Lesson for Alloro's audit / Recognition-Score: any competitor-grading output must stay **factual and sourced** (geo SERP screenshots = evidence), **state methodology in plain language**, and **never frame a named competitor as disparagement**. Also: never the words **"free audit"** (banned + legal exposure), lead with the specific finding. (Source: Notion "Site SEO/AEO/CRO Direction"; the suit itself not independently re-verified, confirm before citing specifics.)
