**Diagnostic law (the core):** find the RATE-LIMITING failure (invisible vs distrusted vs friction) and treat THAT. Never reflex to "more leads", pouring acquisition into a leaking bucket is the #1 waste.

## Cited mechanism per stage
- **Getting found:** discovery spreads across surfaces (Google Search ~45% / Maps ~20% / social ~14% / AI ~5% and rising, BrightLocal 2025); local pack = Relevance + Distance + Prominence, primary CATEGORY the strongest single lever; ~68% zero-click; AI Overviews ~halve clicks (Pew); AI answers pull cited/structured content (Princeton GEO +30-115%); ChatGPT reads Bing/Yelp, not GBP.
- **Trust (credence goods, Darby & Karni):** buyers judge the PROVIDER, not the work; integrity dominates early (McKnight); ~46% judge credibility on visual design (Stanford/Fogg); first impression ~50ms (Lindgaard); review sweet spot 4.2-4.5 (Spiegel); negativity bias (Baumeister); honesty-against-self-interest (Sheridan) is the hardest-to-fake signal; verdict beats menu (choice overload, Iyengar).
- **Friction (Fogg B=MAP):** by this stage motivation is present, so failure = ability/prompt, NOT price; ~70% cart abandonment is friction not price (Baymard); 1->3s load = +32% bounce (Google); the local terminal node = click-to-call.
- **Advocacy (loop-close):** WOM most-trusted (Nielsen 83%); referred customers ~16% more valuable (Schmitt); earned by peak/end (Kahneman); soliciting DISCOUNTS the premium (Wirtz), so Alloro's lane is presence-side only (honest expectation-setting, surface/reply organic reviews, provider-to-provider referral health), never manufacture.
