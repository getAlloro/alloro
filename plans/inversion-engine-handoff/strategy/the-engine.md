## What Alloro is
**An expert physician of business health.** Not a dentist's marketing tool, not a vertical SEO app: a
physician who *diagnoses AND treats* the health of any local-service business. (This IS the locked
moat, "tells the owner the #1 move AND does it," verdict to done, stated in medical terms.)
- **The engine IS the value.** The system that creates drive, momentum, and power for every business
  plugged in is not overhead sitting behind the value; it *is* the value, delivered at scale instead
  of one bespoke job at a time.
- **A diesel engine:** high torque (lifts the ones struggling under load, not just the easy ones);
  universal (any local-service business, near-identical lift, the "identical lift for anyone");
  immediate (from the moment it's turned on).

## The intellectual foundation: pattern-recognition of universal pathophysiology
- The world is patterns. Google's algorithm is undisclosed but *lived-in and talked about* by the
  practitioners, so the pattern is recoverable. Same with what makes a local business healthy or sick.
- **Organize by mechanism, not vertical.** A cardiologist doesn't file "the plumber's heart" and "the
  lawyer's heart" separately; the heart works the same. The organ-systems of a business, *found /
  chosen / booked / retained / the economics that keep it alive*, are the same in a dental office and a
  law firm. The vertical is just the patient in the chair.
- **Vertical-neutral** (already canon: Master Recipe expansion thesis "dental is the beachhead, not the
  ceiling"; Business Clarity for every local-service owner; resonance-copy rule #8). Marketing picks
  the vertical; the engine is universal. It should be **self-applicable to Alloro itself**: a physician
  who can't diagnose its own health can't diagnose a stranger's.

## The fuel and the architecture (the SpaceX frame)
- The skills, the research library, the disciplines are **fuel + architecture**, the difference between
  a 50cc moped and a SpaceX rocket: knowledge, experience, theory, design.
- **SpaceX tests off of it.** Stand on the proven physics (every prior rocket, every proven company
  playbook) as a guideline, then *improve and optimize* your own. Confirming you use their lessons is
  table stakes; the edge is the part you *add*.
- **Understand the entire system first.** You can't build pathophysiology on live patients; you study
  the whole system, then treat. Falcon 1 was minimal in scope but built on *complete* physics. So:
  understand the full system of the engine you're turning on *before* it touches a paying owner.

## The build: textbook + exam (the physician's education)
- The library today is deep chapters (861 sourced dossiers, 427 of them 10KB+) but **no spine**: no
  synthesized pathophysiology. The build is the *textbook*: chapters by mechanism, gaps researched to
  completion (research-store-first so we don't re-derive).
- **The exam is the part that matters most and doesn't exist yet:** a set of real businesses with known
  outcomes plus the correct diagnosis for each, that the engine must pass. It does double duty, it
  *certifies* the knowledge is correct (not a plausible-but-wrong pattern) AND proves the engine
  *learned* it. It is also the **surpass mechanism**: AI runs the deliberate-practice loop off it
  faster than any bootstrapped competitor can, turning *using* the playbook into *becoming* it. (This
  is doctrine mechanism #5, `alloro-eval`, made concrete; also the SpaceX pre-launch sim, test the
  diagnosis against a known answer before it's pointed at a paying owner.)

## The method: measure your way into physics (exam + simulation, honestly)
Grounded in the 2026-07-06 research battery (medical-AI diagnosis + eval-engineering + simulation
methods; stored in `research/`). The truth all four veins converge on: **Alloro has no validated
growth-physics yet, so it must MEASURE its way into one.** SpaceX's sims work on validated physics;
ours don't exist until we've measured enough levers.
- **The wedge (what the engine claims):** *sub-visual detection*, read the pathophysiology in the
  owner's OWN routine data and surface what they can't see (the AI-ECG reading a "normal" tracing as a
  hidden signature). NOT "we analyze faster / we've seen more" (the hype claim that invites an argument
  and that medicine shows collapses off-distribution).
- **The validity gate:** no discovered pattern drives a verdict until it holds OUT-OF-SAMPLE, on a
  different account/time than it was found on. Catches leakage, shortcut-learning, and the
  confident-but-wrong proxy (score_gap = 100 - score).
- **The exam is a smoke detector, not a certificate.** A 3-4 case exam (the current customers + Alloro)
  can only *falsify a broken engine*; it cannot certify "good" (4/4 is statistically consistent with
  ~50% true accuracy). Contamination is the sharpest trap: if the engine's knowledge is authored while
  looking at those customers, testing on them is worth ~zero. Fix: blind-locked answer key +
  criterion-level rubric + **prospective cases** (a dated, locked diagnosis on each NEW customer before
  the outcome is known). Certification of "good" begins with prospective, not retrospective.
- **Simulation, honestly:** at this stage, *pre-launch* = a Monte Carlo range-forecast whose job is to
  CATCH ISSUES (assumptions shown, ranges not points); *post-launch* = CausalImpact to MEASURE what a
  shipped lever moved. Every measured lever tightens the next forecast. Never sell a correlation
  forecast as a causal one.
- **So Falcon 1 = ship the loop, catch the red flags, measure every lever honestly, and let validated
  physics + certification accrue prospectively.** The fleet (real causal simulation, a certified
  engine) is *earned* by measurement, not claimed.

## The sequence (already the Master Recipe's order)
1. **Build the diesel engine, make it undeniable.** Undeniable is a *number*, not a vibe, set against
   the proven players' real bars (Owner +$10k/mo; Birdeye's owner-watches-the-number-move; ServiceTitan
   +17%). For Alloro: a true, attributable result at least one of the 3 customers can SEE in their own
   numbers and vouch for, on a cadence.
2. **THEN acquisition** (Master Recipe: Activate to Prove to Acquire; don't pour acquisition into a
   leaking bucket). The magic, the Apple/Nike feeling, is *marketing + messaging*, and it ships only
   *after* the engine is proven, or you break trust you can't rebuild.

## The moat (why it must be defensible, especially with AI)
- No one is doing this today, but once Alloro makes noise a bootstrapped competitor can't be allowed to
  catch up. **Alloro needs moats** (study the Birdeye / Zapier playbooks). The named edge:
  attribution-owned proof the owner watches in their own data; quiet-professional trust in a lemons
  market; AI-native speed (the deliberate-practice exam loop).
- Stakes: 96% of small businesses fail, yet more become unicorns than ever. The pathophysiology that
  separates the two is exactly what the engine reads.

## The endgame (Falcon 1, not the fleet)
Once the engine is validated, proven, and running consistently, the fun begins: a company to grow, and
a system to watch and learn from. The unicorn pattern is clear, **most own a workflow and have fintech
integrated.** Alloro does *neither* today, and that is the honest, correct state: the *how* is not
obvious yet, and canon already says so (the Master Recipe's expansion thesis routes the fintech half
through PAYMENTS as the universal substrate, *after* the beachhead is won: "no new product line until
the core is proven").
- **This build is Falcon 1, not the final fleet.** The self-proving loop is the first vehicle, not the endgame.
- **But it is the wedge toward the fleet, not a detour from it.** To prove the loop, Alloro must own the
  *attribution* (which the repo does not have yet, it must be built, per Card 2). Owning "which patient
  came from where" is the first brick of a *workflow*, the proto-system-of-record. That is how the "how"
  becomes obvious: build the loop, own the attribution data, and the path to owning the workflow, then
  integrating fintech, clarifies from a position of data, not a guess. The wedge installs the rail the
  fleet rides (the Weave / Podium pattern from the library).
- **Vertical-neutrality is a fintech-moat requirement, not a preference.** In the dental lane, "own
  fintech" means insurance / RCM / claims (HIPAA + PHI, the dental-corner, per canon). Across broad
  local-service it means PAYMENTS, the clean universal substrate every local business takes. So
  verticalizing the *product* into "the dental tool" forecloses the entire fintech endgame. The
  precision: **beachhead the go-to-market, never verticalize the physician.** Marketing can aim at
  dental for focus; the product / engine / identity stays the universal local-service professional.
  Corner the marketing and you can un-corner later; corner the product and you've sold the endgame for
  a beachhead.

### The operational-empathy moat + adoption (2026-07-07, Corey)
Competitors (Owner.com, Podium, Weave, Birdeye, agencies) build for the owner-BUYER and for how THEY the vendor run a business (websites, marketing, reviews). They do NOT build for how the practice OPERATES or for the staff-USERS (front desk, practice manager, hygienist). So staff resist new software (more work, threatens their process), and staff resistance is what stops the doctor from moving forward. **Software dies at the front desk, not in the demo.** The deeper moat: understand how the ICP actually operates, so what Alloro builds, the staff will actually USE. Adoption is the hidden gate the whole category misses, and it is hard to replicate (it takes operational empathy, not more features).
