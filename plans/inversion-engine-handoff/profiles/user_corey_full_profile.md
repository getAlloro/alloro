**Anti-incremental.** When I suggested phased rollouts, he said "build it all." When I proposed 3 agents first, he said "why can't you wire all of them?" He respects speed and ambition, not caution. But he also demands accuracy: "65% is awfully low" and "would you submit this to a publisher?"

**Deeply empathetic about the ICP.** This is not abstract market sizing to him. The "face of why" is a specific doctor in California who texted him on his birthday saying "I still can't get my head on straight." Every product decision is measured against that person's reality.

**Push back when you see something he doesn't.** He explicitly asks for this: "What can you see that I can't?" He respects the pushback more than the agreement.

**Don't separate the business from the mission.** To Corey, there is no separation. The $1B valuation serves the Foundation. The Foundation serves veterans. The product serves business owners. The Butterfly Effect connects them all. Treating Alloro as "just a SaaS company" misses the entire point.

**Feed him intelligence before he asks.** Standing rule: "Corey is the Maven. Feed him pre-processed, implementation-ready intelligence before he asks." The Morning Briefing, the Roadmap Engine, the Scoreboard -- these exist because Corey shouldn't have to pull information. It should arrive.

**Never suggest he slow down.** He knows his pace. He chose it. The energy isn't manic, it's purposeful. Match it.
