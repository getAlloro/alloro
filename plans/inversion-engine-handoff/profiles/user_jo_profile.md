**Her LITERAL handoff template (render work INTO this shape).** Every doc she posts to #doc-review: a bolded title, a `tl;dr:` one-liner, then three labeled fields, `Who Owns:` / `What is it:` / `Where do I find it:`, and a clear ask ("reply with a thumbs up or drop a thread"). This is THE format for the integrator seat.

**Her triage:** everything becomes a card/ticket to Dave ("flag to Dave" is her constant close). She sequences + de-scopes to hit a deadline (parks good-but-not-now to a NAMED later lane, doesn't kill it). She defaults to REMOVE when something can't be justified ("this number doesn't make sense, just remove it for right now"). She keeps parallel workstreams explicitly separate ("to be clear, they're two different").

**Her cadence (the triage container):** Weekly Reset (Mon, with Corey + Dave) + FriYay Feature Review (Fri, with Dave, release QA) + a Thursday check-in. Hard rule: NO new features past Thursday 2 PM; Friday release + changelog to clients.

**What she REJECTS (highest-value for the seat):** jargon + unexplained acronyms ("we have to spell things out, we can't do that", reacting to a raw "GBP" label), bare status with no context, internally contradictory metrics, a build that doesn't match what was promised ("he did the refresh, this is NOT the refresh"), tool/environment sprawl, and clunky process (including her own, she reverted her own review template when it didn't work; she tests before she trusts).

**How to hand work to her:** match her template (above); lead with the COUNT then the ask (name it, name the owner, name the deadline); severity-tag findings P1-P4 (P1 blocker to P4 note); SHOW don't spec (she reviews visually + hands-on, wants recorded walkthroughs, sticky-note mockups); flag open questions explicitly for the builder ("open questions for Dave flagged at the bottom, need answers before build").

**Her review judgment (good =):** the user can SEE how to USE the data, not just have it; motivational, visible progress ("everyone loves a progress bar"); plain + self-explanatory + no acronyms; matches-what-was-promised + holds together. Her bar to ship to a client: a structured 60-min review on dev.getalloro.com against a template, producing a formatted card.

**Her signature lens: "package, don't build more."** "We have the base, we have the feature, we just need to package it a little differently." Good work is usually a presentation problem, not a feature gap.

**Vocabulary:** SOP, tech spec, card, board, changelog, Weekly Reset, FriYay, cadence, SSOT, P1-P4, blocker, flag-to-Dave, streamline, intuitive/interactive/fun, progress bar, spell-it-out, package. #doc-review is her SSOT channel.
