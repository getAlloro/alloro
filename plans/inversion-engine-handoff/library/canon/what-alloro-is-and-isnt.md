## What Alloro Is
Alloro is Business Clarity software for local service business owners.
The category doesn't exist yet. We're creating it.
**Alloro is your business picture.** Before Alloro, local service business owners are flying blind — their own words, confirmed across the industry. After Alloro, they have a clear picture of how their business looks from the outside, how it's performing on the inside, and where they stand against competitors. The picture updates automatically. They didn't manage any of it.
Every tool a small business can buy today does one of two things: it operates the business (booking, payments, scheduling, CRM) or it promotes the business (ads, social, SEO agencies). What nobody has built — at a price point a local service business can afford, in language they can actually understand — is the thing that shows them how their business is actually doing and what to do about it.
Business owners are suffering not because they're bad at their craft, but because nobody ever taught them to run a business. They started with a skill and accidentally bought a second job they never wanted. The stress travels — to their staff, their patients, their family. Alloro gives them the space to be who they actually are. To go back to the reason they chose a life of service.
We sell freedom. Not software.

## The Three Beliefs This Is Built On
These are not marketing claims. They are the foundation. If any of them is wrong, the company is wrong.
**Belief 1 — The suffering is real and measurable.**
This is not anecdotal. 72% of entrepreneurs report experiencing mental health concerns (UC Berkeley). 56% feel completely alone when solving problems and making decisions (Ramsey Solutions, 2023). 42% experienced burnout in the last year — that is approximately 14 million small business owners (Ramsey Solutions). 82% struggle with cash flow consistently. These numbers come from UC Berkeley, Ramsey Solutions, and the Federal Reserve Small Business Credit Survey. The people Alloro serves are not struggling because they lack ambition or talent. They are struggling because nobody taught them to run a business and nobody is watching the numbers with them.
**Belief 2 — The solution format already exists.**
Hormozi has it. The Shark Tank judges have it. Marcus Lemonis has it. The format for reading a business, identifying what is wrong, and knowing what to do next is known and repeatable. It is the same questions every time: revenue, customers, cost to acquire, lifetime value, referral sources, trends. The problem is not that the framework doesn't exist. The problem is delivery. It requires a seat in the room — a consultant, an advisor, a partner — that most local service business owners cannot afford and do not know how to find.
**Belief 3 — The bet we are making.**
This one is not settled. It is the thesis the company is built on.
If AI can interpret a business's own data the way a seasoned advisor would — not just display it, but read it, weight it, and tell the owner what it means and what to do next before they know to ask — then Alloro can be that advisor at scale for the 1.9 million US business owners who are currently running without one. That is the Google Moment. Before Google, information existed but required expertise to access. Before Alloro, business clarity exists but requires a seat in the room that most owners can't get.
This has not been proven yet. Every decision in the Reflect roadmap is either validating this bet or telling us where it breaks. The product outline's job is to hold us accountable to the bet, not paper over it.

## Who Alloro Is For
**The ICP:** A local service business owner who is skilled at their craft, undertrained as a business owner, and stuck in the valley between knowing the business is hard and not yet knowing what to do about it.
They get most of their business through referrals or word of mouth. They've never had clear visibility into the health of what they've built. They feel isolated — no peers to compare with, no advisor watching the numbers. They're not failing. They're waterboarded. There's a difference.
**For our beachhead:** Dental specialists — endodontists and orthodontists. High procedure value, referral-dependent growth model, and a complete absence of good business intelligence in the category. The referral layer is the moat.
**The five problems every local service business owner has:**
1. **Opacity** — they don't know how their business looks to the outside world
2. **Untrained for the job** — skilled at the craft, not at running a business
3. **Unmeasurable spend** — they can't tell if what they're doing for marketing actually works
4. **Isolation** — no peers, no trusted advisor, no one watching while they sleep
5. **Always behind** — competitors figured out something they haven't yet
These five don't change by vertical. The vocabulary changes. The data sources change. The problems are universal. Alloro Connect addresses 1, 3, and 5. Alloro Reflect addresses 2, 4, and the business side of 3.
**Green flags — this is the right prospect:**
- Relies on referrals or word of mouth for most new business
- Has never seen a clear picture of their market position vs. competitors
- Doesn't have a staff member dedicated to business analytics
- Has tried agencies or DIY marketing and couldn't tell if it worked
- Feels like the business owns them, not the other way around
**Who Alloro is not for:**
- Practices that have built in-house analytics staff — they've outgrown the problem we solve
- Businesses looking for a marketing agency to run campaigns
- Operations that need booking, scheduling, or client management software
- Anyone who already knows their numbers and has a trusted advisor watching them

## The Operating Principle — Show the Work
Two equally-weighted jobs. Do the work the customer pays for, and surface that work as proof, in named, dollar-anchored, owner-readable form.
This is the operating principle that turns the North Stars into product. NS1 says the value must be undeniable to every customer. Value is only undeniable when the customer can see it. Capability that runs invisibly is capability that does not exist for the customer. Empirically, the Labor Illusion (Buell and Norton, Harvard Business School, 2011) measures customers valuing services 22 percent more when they see the work happening, even at identical outcomes. Operational Transparency (Buell, HBR 2019) is the design discipline that makes this systematic. The 22 percent is not a marketing lift. It is the gap between Alloro doing real work and Alloro getting credit for it.
The operational implication is structural. Every backend capability requires a corresponding customer-facing surface. Every surface requires the underlying capability or it cannot ship. Capability without a surface is wasted engineering. A surface without capability is a lie. The two ship together, or neither ships.
This is the doctrine that prevents the rave gap from reopening. The May 22 Holy Shit audit revealed five fully-built capabilities that never reached a customer surface: Monday Email, Ranking Leaderboard, Scheduler Heartbeat, GP Drift Detection, Site Preview Card. The Wave 2 sprint closes those five mechanically. The Show the Work principle closes them permanently, by making "backend ships without surface" a non-shippable state.
**The Show the Work filter.** A feature passes when (a) the customer can see the work happen, (b) the customer can read the work in their own language, and (c) the work is named, dollar-anchored, or specifically attributed where possible. A feature that scores no on any of the three is not done. This filter sits alongside the North Stars filter. A feature must pass both filters to ship.

## Alloro Connect
**What it is:** The visibility and acquisition layer. Alloro Connect makes a local service business findable, credible, and chosen by people who are already searching for what they do.
**What makes it different from Squarespace or a basic agency:**
A Squarespace site exists. An Alloro Connect site converts. The difference is everything that happens before the site goes live and after. Before: Alloro asks the right questions to generate copy in the owner's actual voice — not a template filled with their name. After: Alloro monitors how the site performs against local competitors, tracks GBP content freshness, filters every form submission through AI spam detection, and surfaces what to fix next. A Squarespace user has to know what to do. An Alloro client doesn't have to think about it.
The agency comparison: the average dental specialty agency charges $3,500/month and manages campaigns. Alloro is $2,000/month and manages the foundation that makes any future marketing work. No campaigns. No ad spend. The organic infrastructure that compounds.
**What it includes:**
- **Alloro Website Engine** — SEO-optimized, fast, US-hosted. Built to convert, not just to exist. AI-assisted copy generation from the owner's actual voice. The site reflects who the business is, not a template with their logo dropped in.
- **Alloro SEO + AEO Engine** — Automatically optimized for Google and for AI search. When someone asks ChatGPT or Perplexity "best endodontist near me," Alloro-optimized practices surface. This is not optional in the current search environment.
- **Alloro Protect** — 10-layer AI spam filtering on every form. No CAPTCHA. No spam in the inbox. Every real lead gets through; every vendor pitch and bot gets stopped. Auto-applied to every site. The owner never thinks about it.
- **Alloro Vision** — Proprietary analytics. Which pages are working, which aren't, what's driving traffic. In language the owner can read without a developer.
- **GBP Management** — Google Business Profile monitored and maintained. Content freshness is tracked automatically. Google penalizes profiles that go dormant past 60 days — Alloro prevents this without the owner having to manage it.
- **WHICH Engine** — Competitive visibility. How this practice ranks against local competitors on reviews, profile completeness, and search presence. The answer to "see who's beating you on Google" — made specific and named.
- **HOW Engine** — Not just where you stand, but exactly what to do to climb. Prioritized, plain-language recommendations based on your specific gaps against your specific competitors.
**Undeniable value:** A business owner opens Alloro Connect and knows, in one glance, how their practice shows up when someone searches for their service in their market. They know who's beating them, why, and what to fix first. Their site is optimized, their forms are protected, their Google profile is current — and they didn't manage any of it.
The math is not close. Endodontists spend $1,300 to $3,210 per month on advertising alone trying to compensate for weak organic presence. The only named agency in this space with published pricing charges $2,000 to $5,000 per month for less — no AEO, no competitive intelligence, no spam protection. Alloro Connect at $2,000 per month costs less than being invisible, and less than the agency that would try to fix it. One additional patient per month from better local search visibility covers the annual cost on its own. The value is not subjective. The math works before anything else is counted.

## Alloro Reflect
**What it is:** The business clarity layer. Alloro Reflect takes the data a local service business already has and translates it into the numbers every successful entrepreneur tracks — delivered in plain language, with the interpretation included.
The standard Reflect is built to meet: what Hormozi does in the hot seat, what Shark Tank judges do in the first five minutes of every pitch — knowing your numbers, not because you calculated them, but because someone read the data and told you what it means. Reflect is not fully there yet. What it delivers today is the foundation. The roadmap section below is the path to close the gap.
This is Belief 3 in practice. Every build decision in Reflect is a test of whether the bet is real.
**What it delivers today (dental specialists):**
- Referral source tracking — who is sending patients, trending up or down, and what changed
- Production trend analysis — month-over-month practice performance from PMS data
- Referral source ranking — which relationships matter most to revenue
- AI recommendations — based on patterns in the data, here's what to prioritize
**What it must deliver next — the revenue gap:** *(Roadmap — not current state. Do not share with clients as live capability.)*
Right now, Reflect shows activity. It should show business clarity. The difference:
*Activity data:* "Dr. Smith sent 8 referrals this month. That's down from 11 last month."
*Business clarity:* "Dr. Smith sent 8 referrals this month. At your average procedure value, that's approximately $12,000 in revenue from this relationship. His referrals are down 27% over 6 months. At that trend, you're looking at $43,200 in annual revenue at risk. Here's what to do."
The revenue connection — translating referral counts and trends into dollar figures — is the step between a dashboard and a business advisor. This is the next build priority for Reflect. The data is already there. The interpretation is what's missing.
**The four Rs Reflect closes:**
Hormozi's customer success framework: Retain, Review, Refer, Resell. Alloro has Review (GBP monitoring) and Refer (referral tracking). What's next:
- **Retain:** Are referred patients converting to procedures and returning? The referral loop currently closes at intake. It should close at revenue.
- **Trajectory:** "At your current trend, here's where your practice will be in 12 months." Snapshot data informs. Trajectory data creates urgency.
**What Reflect looks like for non-dental verticals:**
This is not a new data collection problem. Every local service business already has this data — it lives in whatever tool they already use to run their business. The question Jo asked in the Weekly Reset (May 18) was "where would they even have this information?" The answer: they already have it. Alloro doesn't collect it. Alloro reads it and interprets it.
Gloss Genius exports client lists, appointment history, and revenue. Jobber exports customer records, completed jobs, and revenue. Square exports transactions and customer data. QuickBooks exports everything. Housecall Pro, Mindbody, Vagaro, Booksy — every vertical-specific booking and transaction system has a CSV export. The barber, the landscaper, the chiropractor, the attorney — they all have this data. It's just sitting in a system they never think about analytically.
Alloro Reflect for non-dental verticals reads that CSV export and produces the same read Hormozi does in a hot seat. The four questions he asks every business owner are answerable from any booking or transaction export:
1. How many customers did you get this period? (new vs. returning split)
2. Where did they come from? (referral, search, returning, other — extracted from source field or inferred)
3. How many came back? (repeat rate, recency, at-risk flag)
4. How much did they spend? (revenue total, per customer, per service type)
With those four inputs, Alloro Reflect produces: customer acquisition trend, churn risk flags (customers who haven't returned in X weeks at their normal cadence), revenue concentration risk (what percentage comes from the top 10 clients), and plain-English recommendations on what to do about each.
**This is not a CRM.** A CRM manages the relationships — sends emails, logs calls, tracks deals. Alloro reads the data that the CRM or booking system already produces and interprets it in a way the owner can act on. The landscaper doesn't need Alloro to manage their customer relationships. They need Alloro to tell them that three of their top 10 accounts haven't ordered since March, and at their average job size, that's $4,200 in annual revenue that's drifting. Their existing tool has the data. Alloro has the interpretation.
**The build question for Dave (not a vision item — a specific technical question):** Can the existing PMS CSV parser be generalized to accept a standard customer-plus-revenue CSV from any source, not just dental PMS formats? The dental parser reads referral source, patient count, production total, and dates. The universal parser reads customer ID, revenue amount, service date, and source. The interpretation layer is identical. If the parser can be generalized, non-dental Reflect is a configuration change, not a rebuild.
**Stage gates for non-dental Reflect:**
- Stage 0 (current): Dental PMS CSV upload → referral tracking and production analysis. Works today.
- Stage 1 (next): Generalized CSV upload with customer, revenue, date, and source fields → same analysis for any vertical. One Alloro client in a non-dental vertical validates this.
- Stage 2: Direct integrations with Gloss Genius, Jobber, Square, and QuickBooks APIs eliminate the manual CSV upload entirely. The data flows automatically.
**Undeniable value:** A business owner opens their Monday morning email from Alloro and sees — before the day starts — exactly where their practice stands, which relationships are healthy, which are at risk, and what one thing to do about it. Not what happened. What it means and what to do. They didn't wonder about their business once over the weekend.

## The Second Act — Embedded Finance
**Subscription is year one. Embedded finance is what turns Alloro from successful vertical SaaS into a category-defining unicorn.**
The structural pattern is unambiguous across every vertical SaaS company that has reached $1B+ in valuation. Toast: payments and fintech are now the majority of gross profit. ServiceTitan: 25% of revenue at IPO was usage-based fintech, generating roughly $170M ARR and growing faster than the subscription core. Procore: integrated payments and project financing. Shopify: merchant solutions (payments, capital, shipping) account for 73% of total revenue. Every vertical SaaS company that hit unicorn shares this characteristic. **Own the workflow first. Then monetize the money flowing through it.**
Alloro reads the data substrate every embedded finance product needs. PMS exports, referral source mapping, production trend data, GBP activity, review velocity, form submission patterns. That is the same data that powers underwriting decisions, cash flow predictions, and revenue smoothing for the businesses Alloro serves. The customer already pays for the visibility. The business model expansion is making the money flowing through their practice work harder, with Alloro as the intelligent intermediary.
This is not a feature ask. It is the structural commitment that locks Alloro on the unicorn path. The thesis is written here, not yet built, not yet shipped. It becomes a build priority when three preconditions hold: subscription product-market fit is undeniable (current sprint closes this), customer trust is durable (year one), and the data substrate is mature enough to underwrite (year one to year two).
### The second act, named
**Alloro Capital (working name).** Practice Cash Flow Smoothing for local service businesses. The Toast Capital model applied to dental specialty first, then to every vertical Alloro serves. Of the four candidate products listed below, this is THE second act for Alloro. The other three sequence as future iterations of the same underwriting infrastructure, not parallel bets.
Why this one first. Alloro already collects the data substrate needed to underwrite production-based advances: PMS production trends, referral source health, GBP signals as forward indicators. With that substrate, Alloro forecasts the practice's next 60 to 90 days of production with precision and offers a working capital advance against the forecast. Repayment runs through incoming insurance reimbursements and patient payments at a structured take rate.
The structural twin is exact. Toast Capital advances merchant cash against forecasted card sales. Alloro Capital advances practice cash against forecasted production. Same underwriting logic. Same customer relationship. Same channel cost (existing subscription customers, no new acquisition cost). Different data substrate.
The second-act roadmap sequence:
- Year 2 to 3: Alloro Capital. Built first because the data substrate already exists and the cash flow pain is highest in the beachhead.
- Year 3 to 4: Equipment and expansion financing layered on the same underwriting infrastructure. Same model, larger ticket size.
- Year 4 to 5: Referral guarantee products as a higher-margin upsell. Category-defining; no structural twin yet exists.
- Year 5+: Payments processing as the integration layer that owns the transaction flow. Highest infrastructure lift, highest unit economics, last to ship.
One product first. Built on substrate Alloro already has. The other three follow as the same underwriting engine extends to new ticket sizes and risk profiles.

Detail on each of the four products. Alloro Capital (named above) is product #1. Products 2 to 4 are the sequenced follow-ons.

### The story within the dashboard
The chapters below are not 10 features. They are three acts of a story the business owner experiences when they open Alloro. The story matches what every local service business owner already needs answered, in the order they need it answered.
**Opening -- The problem.** A local service business owner opens Alloro for the first time. They got here because they have been flying blind. ### Research foundation
The chapter takeaways below are anchored to six named frameworks from behavioral economics and service design research. These are the operative theories the team uses to make product decisions.
**The Labor Illusion** (Buell and Norton, Harvard Business School, Management Science, 2011). Customers value services more when they see the work happening, even when results are identical. The mechanism is perceived effort triggering reciprocity. Applied to Alloro: every chapter must make invisible work visible. Spam filters, GBP refreshes, ranking changes, AI overview placements. The work happens. The client never sees it. That is the structural cause of the value perception gap.
**Operational Transparency** (Buell, Harvard Business Review, 2019). The deliberate design of windows into and out of operations. Field experiments showed 22 percent gains in customer-reported quality and 19 percent gains in efficiency. The Domino's Pizza Tracker is the case study: same pizza, transparency drove customer satisfaction from 69 to 81 percent and 40 percent year-over-year sales growth in some regions. Alloro is in the same position. The data already exists in the system. The work is to surface it.
**The IKEA Effect** (Norton, Mochon, Ariely, Journal of Consumer Psychology, 2012). When customers contribute labor to a product, they value the result 63 percent more than identical pre-made versions. Mechanism: effort justification and ownership. Applied to Alloro: every dashboard surface should invite one-click action from the owner. Approve, confirm, publish, accept. The labor is the loyalty.
**The Rivalry Reference Effect** (Berendt, Uhrich, Borah, Kilduff, Journal of Marketing Research, 2024). Referencing a named rival (versus a generic competitor) in brand messaging significantly increases engagement. The shared-history relationship between specific named entities is what drives the response. Applied to Alloro: never say "a competitor." Say "Dr. Sackowitz." The named rival is the empirical operative element, not a stylistic choice.
**Negativity Bias and Loss Aversion** (Kahneman and Tversky, foundational behavioral economics). Losses register roughly twice as strongly as equivalent gains. Caroline noticed "board certified" was wrong (loss frame) and did not notice she ranked above her rival (gain frame). This is wiring, not failure. Applied to Alloro: gains must be designed with at least equal visual and emotional weight to losses, or they will not register at all.
**The Pricing Iceberg** (industry framework, multiple sources). Most of a service's value sits below the customer's waterline: expertise, R&D, problems prevented, support infrastructure. The prescription is to deliberately move more of the iceberg above the waterline through positioning and in-product evidence. Alloro at $2,000 per month against a defensible retail stack value of $10,000 to $20,000 is the textbook iceberg pricing problem. The fix is in the product, not the pitch.
**Industry baseline (vcita 2025 SMB Digital Adoption Report).** 60 percent of small businesses that churned from their marketing agency in the past year cited not feeling they were getting an adequate return on investment as the primary reason. That is double the 2023 figure. The value perception gap is the dominant SMB churn driver industry-wide, not a Caroline-specific or Alloro-specific failure. The frameworks above are the documented mechanism by which the gap closes.
