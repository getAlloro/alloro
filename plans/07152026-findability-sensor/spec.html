A5 · Slice 1 — The Findability Sensor

The honest instrument under the fuel gauge. Replace today's single, flickering rank point with stable, proximity-aware sampling of the service searches that bring new customers, across the practice's real area. Slice 1 of the scientist-loop engine (sensor → gauge → hypothesize → act → watch → learn). Not a grid the owner reads — the sampling is plumbing.

Plan: 07152026-findability-sensor  ·  Funnel: Stage A (get found), item A5, slice 1  ·  Proven example: Local Falcon (geo-grid) — meet the instrument, exceed the experience
Grounded (do not re-derive): research/rank-geo-grid-mechanism.md · research/rank-findability-voc-and-fuel-gauge.md · strategy/self-proving-loop.md · proving-simulation-design.md (E2)
Evidence: DIRECTIONAL · MEASUREMENT — a repeatable estimate, not truth-of-position; rank→revenue is lore. A mirror, never a #1 promise.

Why

The business is the owner's minivan, and Alloro handed them a broken fuel gauge for findability — so they distrust it. Primary VoC: Saif (1Endo) found the rank feature "unreliable for daily use"; Garrison googles himself every day; Artful almost cancelled over it (churn). The root cause, named on Saif's call and confirmed by the research: rank is "inconsistent, largely influenced by proximity to competitors" — Google's dominant local factor is distance, so a single sample point flickers and systematically flatters (the owner searches from their own desk, the point distance most favors them, and sees a false #1).

You can't build a trustworthy gauge on a broken sensor. Slice 1 fixes the sensor: sample many points, for the right keywords, and aggregate into a stable, honest reading. The confusing grid stays internal plumbing; the owner never decodes it.

Success = the owner checks less, not somewhere else (Rev 2). Garrison googles himself every day; the win is not moving that daily anxious check into an Alloro screen — it is ending the need for it. A gauge that flickers day-to-day (the single-point failure mode) would just relocate the compulsion. So the sensor's job is stability the owner can trust enough to stop looking: same-spot, same-grid, trend-over-time sampling on a cadence Alloro runs — a reading calm enough that "I already know where I sit" replaces "let me check again." Reduced checking is the design success signal every downstream slice inherits; a sensor that produces a jumpy number has failed even if every metric is computed correctly.

The full architecture (context — this slice builds the first box)

The scientist loop (Corey, 2026-07-15) — the measure-and-act engine A5 becomes the brain of:

Sensor → Reader/Diagnostic → Hypothesizer + Lever-Router → Actor (pulls A2/A4/A1, owner-gated) → Watcher (proving / E2) → Learner (fleet moat, NS2) — with the owner-facing gauge as the translated read.

This slice = the Sensor only. The Reader, Hypothesizer, Actor, and the owner Gauge are subsequent slices; the Watcher/Learner is E2 (already designed — proving-simulation-design.md + self-proving-loop.md; don't re-derive). Everything downstream reads the sensor, so it is the honest first cut.

The Learner (fleet moat) is aspirational, not present — say so (Rev 2). At today's N ≈ 3–4 customers there is no cross-customer fleet to learn from. So nothing this slice ships — and no copy above it — may claim Alloro "learns across practices" / "the fleet gets smarter." What is true today and all we claim: the sensor learns your practice's own map over time (its own time-series, its own service area). The cross-customer flywheel (NS2) is a real future once N is large enough that a winning pattern on one map generalizes to another — the persisted per-practice time-series is deliberately the substrate that will feed it — but until then it is a designed direction, not a live capability. Overclaiming a fleet-moat we don't have would be the exact Value-#6 violation this engine exists to avoid.

What — in scope (the honest sensor)

Reuse first (verified against code + the graded research).

  • The per-point rank call — getSearchPositionViaSerpApiMaps (service.serpapi-maps.ts) already runs one keyword at one @lat,lng,zoom vantage (exactly the geo-grid's per-pin call, per the research). The sensor calls it at many vantages. Reuse the grid/geo utils (util.competitor-geo.ts, util.competitor-radius-selection.ts) for point generation.
  • Keyword demand — A1's shipped GSC→content loop already surfaces the org's real top Google queries. The sensor picks the keywords to track from that real demand + the practice's service list — never the owner guessing.
  • Pipeline + persistence shape — mirror the practice-ranking pipeline + the ai-seo-audit persistence pattern (run → execute → persist).

The sensor

  • Keyword selection (service-not-name). Choose the service keyword-families that bring new customers ("orthodontist near me", "Invisalign [city]") — NOT the brand-name search (ranking #1 for your own name is the flattering vanity trap; it's people who already know you). Derived from GSC demand (A1) + the service list. A per-location keyword/area config stores the chosen families; it is done-for-you + owner-steerable ("we added Invisalign" / "we opened South Orange" re-aims it) — never a keyword-research panel the owner operates.
  • Geo sampling (the grid, internal). For each tracked keyword, sample rank across an NxN grid of vantage points over the practice's real service area (research default: 7×7 "Goldilocks" per Merchynt; configurable). Each pin = one reused per-point call. Sample during open hours (Paige's honesty upgrade — Google ranks differ open vs closed).
  • Honest aggregation. Per keyword-family, compute SoLV (% of pins in the top 3 — the owner-legible measure per the research) + ARP/ATRP (internal, agency-grade) + the per-pin raw (for the future map). One honest headline the gauge can translate: "a top-3 choice in X% of your area for the searches that bring new patients."
  • Persist a time-series snapshot — per (location, keyword-family, run): SoLV, coverage, per-pin ranks, competitors-per-pin, observed_at. The record the Reader/Watcher slices read; the "map moving" proof of the self-proving loop.

What — explicitly NOT in this slice

  • No owner-facing grid or gauge UI — the sampling is plumbing; the translated gauge is slice 2.
  • No Reader/Hypothesizer/Actor (the scientist agent) and no Watcher/E2 — later slices; this slice only produces the honest data they read.
  • No rank promise, no "#1", no "keep improving", no rank→revenue claim (Value #6; the research's honesty line; Owner.com's one soft spot we do not copy).
  • No organic (blue-link) rank — this is the local Maps/3-pack, which is what matters for a local service business; label it as such.

Constraints

Honesty (Value #6 + the research's line). Claimable: "here's exactly where you stand, everywhere," "how your map changed since last time," "you own X% of your neighborhood [SoLV]." Forbidden: "we'll make you #1," "your rankings will keep improving," "#1 = $X revenue," "this is exactly what every searcher sees." Every reading is labelled a repeatable estimate/simulation, not ground truth. No gamed signals, ever (the opposite of Jumper).

Anti-fabrication. Persist only what the sampling actually returned (per-pin ranks + the real competitors seen). Never infer a pin's rank; never fabricate coverage; a pin that errored is recorded as unknown, not as "not ranking". Concretely, the reused per-point call returns three states and they must stay distinct forever: ok → a real rank; not_in_top_20 → a genuine "looked, you weren't there" (honest not-ranking, counts against SoLV); api_error → unknown (we could not look — excluded from the SoLV denominator, never scored as absent). Collapsing api_error into "not ranking" is the one bug that quietly manufactures a false reading.

The Watcher's honesty is load-bearing — the moat is the learning rate, not a static playbook (Rev 2). Everything downstream (Reader → Hypothesizer → Actor → Watcher/E2) attributes lever-actions to needle-movement on this sensor. A single false attribution poisons the entire loop: if a scan reports movement that a fabricated pin created, the Hypothesizer learns the wrong lesson, the Actor pulls the wrong lever, and the owner is shown a win that isn't real — and once the owner catches one fake number, the honesty that IS the moat (self-proving-loop.md: "you cannot fake the number and keep the trust") is gone. The defensible asset is not a fixed set of "moves that work" — it is how fast and how honestly the loop learns from real, correctly-attributed readings. That is why the sensor's honesty caps are not cosmetic: they are the foundation the whole compounding advantage stands on. A wrong reading is worse than a missing one.

Sandbox-safety + cost (the real knob). Cost = pins × keywords × SerpApi per scan (a 7×7 × 3 keywords = 147 calls). So grid size, keyword count, and cadence are the cost knobs; the recurring scan is not scheduled in this slice at all (Rev 4) — zero cost, and no dead schedule row pretending otherwise. The schedule lands with the executor slice. Migration additive + reversible; prove DB paths with a rolled-back sandbox transaction. No writes to Google.

  • Per-(location,keyword) failure isolation — one failed pin or keyword never fails the scan; record what succeeded.
  • Edge cases: no service keywords resolvable (no GSC demand yet, no service list) → skip honestly (record nothing, not a fake reading); business with no geo/area → skip; SerpApi unconfigured/over-quota → skip + log, never fabricate; open-hours unknown → note the caveat on the reading.
  • Idempotency — one snapshot per (location, keyword-family, run-date).
  • Lane: Corey+Claude author; build + adversary in the sandbox; PR to dev/dave; never push to dev/dave/main directly.

Risk

  • SerpApi cost scales with pins×keywords. Mitigation: sensible default grid (7×7), small keyword set, cadence knob, and no schedule in this slice (Rev 4) — a scan only runs when invoked.
  • Estimate mistaken for truth. Mitigation: label every reading a repeatable estimate; SoLV is a described observation, never a forecast; no per-searcher "this is what they see" claim.
  • Vanity keyword drift (tracking the name, or agency ARP). Mitigation: keyword selection is service-not-name from real demand; SoLV is the owner headline; ARP/ATRP stay internal.
  • Non-repeatable noise. Mitigation: same-spot, same-grid, open-hours sampling for comparability (Local Falcon's own honesty method); trend-over-time, not a single reading, is the signal.

Tasks

  1. Migration — additive sensor-reading table (per location/keyword/run: SoLV, coverage, per-pin ranks JSON, observed_at) + a keyword/area config table; reversible down. Both uniqueness rules DB-enforced by unique indexes over COALESCE(location_id,-1). No schedule seed (Rev 4).
  2. Keyword selection — a service-not-name keyword-family resolver from GSC demand (A1) + the service list; the done-for-you + owner-steerable config.

  4. Sampling runner — call the reused per-point rank lookup per pin per keyword; isolate failures; a pure aggregator (SoLV/ARP/ATRP) over the pins; persist the idempotent snapshot.
