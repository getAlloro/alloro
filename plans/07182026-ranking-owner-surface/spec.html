Why

The ranking dashboard is the surface the owner actually reads — the "1 ACTION" card and the stat tiles. As Alloro drives the whole A–E funnel, this surface is the CMO's report to the owner (of the local presence funnel — its lane): your funnel health, the one move, and later the lift it produced. It is the door almost every A–E finding must surface through. Today it has two live defects: it speaks hardcoded dental vocabulary to owners in every other vertical (real ICP = any local service business with a GBP + online appointments + higher ticket; dental is one vertical), and its honesty rules live in an LLM prompt, not in code, so a banned generic card ("ask every patient for a review… to protect your #1 ranking") can and did ship to a paying owner. Fix the door first, honestly, or every downstream A–E item lands on a broken surface.

What

Route the ranking recommendation path through the vocabulary system that already exists, and enforce the honesty rules that already exist in prose as real code checks — then add mechanized guards so neither defect can silently return.

  • Resolve the org's orgType at the ranking LLM stage (it is not resolved anywhere in the ranking pipeline today), and tokenize the hardcoded system prompt so it substitutes the org's vocabulary via the built substitutePromptPlaceholders.
  • Enforce the prompt's own ban-list in the deterministic guardrail: reject a recommendation that is generic "get more reviews" with no specific competitor+number, that tells a specialist to match a generalist's review count, or that ties reviews/posts to rank for a non-leader — falling back to the honest, vocab-correct safe copy.
  • Pass orgType into the guardrail so the fallback and honest-action copy also translate.
  • Add two advisory CI checks (vocab-bypass + owner-facing static-claim) to scripts/check-conventions.sh.

Done when: a generic (non-dental) org's ranking prompt and card render "customer/business," never "patient/practice/doctor"; no banned-shape recommendation can reach the owner (falls to the honest backfill); the CI checks flag any new hardcoded owner-facing healthcare noun or unconditioned claim; tsc + tests + check:conventions --strict green.
