Why

Alloro is the CMO of the owner's local presence: it diagnoses, prescribes, runs the right mix of moves, and reports what actually moved. The diagnosis is the part everything else hangs off — get the binding constraint wrong and every downstream move is aimed at the wrong gate.

Today that diagnosis is one line of arithmetic. funnelMath.ts picked the binding constraint as the smallest non-null step percentage, and built the owner-facing headline straight off it. That reads a lone number with no context: it fires on three visitors, it blames page titles when the page ranks on page two, and it never declines to answer. Meanwhile the signals needed to read it properly — how many top queries actually rank on page one, click-through, pages-per-session, bounce — were already assembled by the stage readers and never looked at.

The trained-business-doctor synthesis names the fix precisely: a diagnostic coordination layer that runs the five-move gait across the stage engines and produces one honest verdict. This is brick 1 of that layer — the gait itself, replacing the naive selector behind the existing contract.

What

A pure, tested diagnoseFunnel() that selects the binding constraint by the five-move gait instead of by minimum percentage.

MoveWhat it does here
1 — read the constellationEach step is judged with the paired signals already on the stage metadata (GSC top10QueryCount + click-through, Rybbit pages-per-session + bounce), and the signals used are recorded. A GSC position of 0 is treated as a no-data sentinel, not a first-place ranking. A selection with no independent signal is marked uncorroborated, not dressed up.
2 — trace surface to rootWhen the click-through step is weak because no top query reaches the first page, the root is ranking, not page titles — so the gait refuses to name the metadata and the sentence says where the loss actually is.
3 — feature-vs-bugA weak click-through when none of the top search terms rank in the first ten results is what that model looks like working normally, not a defect. A step whose denominator is under the floor is a small-numbers artifact, not a finding.
4 — one binding constraintThe worst step in the funnel is the candidate, and it is named only if it is diagnosable. Excluding a step never promotes a healthier one. See the global-minimum rule below.
5 — refuse to inventWhen no step survives, it abstains and the headline says what was observed instead of naming an opportunity nobody measured.

Done looks like: the gait is unit-tested move by move, the payload shape is byte-identical, and a wrong verdict can be traced in the logs to the step that produced it.

The global-minimum rule

The candidate is the lowest rate across all steps that have one — not the lowest among the steps that survived the gates. It is named only if it is itself diagnosable; otherwise the gait abstains and reports the exclusion that applied to that worst step.

This is the part that was wrong in the first draft and it is worth recording why, because the failure was invisible from the inside. Selecting the lowest survivor means excluding a step promotes a healthier one. On a realistic account — 1% click-through with nothing on page one, plus a healthy 50% visit-to-lead step — the catastrophic step was correctly excluded and the healthy step was then named "your largest opportunity, only 50% moved through," at which point buildBookableCandidate fired and told the owner the booking step was "where you're losing the most" while 7,920 people were being lost upstream. A wrong verdict plus a false sentence, in the owner-facing payload, on an ordinary account shape. There is now a test named for this that fails if it returns.

Thresholds, and what they claim

One threshold, honestly labelled. The two that were dressed up as "structural facts" are gone:

  • MIN_DIAGNOSABLE_DENOMINATOR = 10 — an operational floor, matching the value buildBookableCandidate already applies, so the project carries one argued number rather than two.
  • Removed — average-position > 10. "Ten results per page" is structural; applying it to GSC's impressions-weighted average across every query is not. A practice ranking #1 for its money terms plus one high-impression generic term on page three averages past 10 while ranking perfectly well. The gate now uses top10QueryCount — how many top queries actually reach the first ten results — which is already on the stage metadata and answers the question the average only appeared to answer.
  • Removed — pages-per-session ≤ 1.05. Invented, with a perverse failure mode: Alloro's own bookable advice is "make your booking form the first thing visitors see," which structurally produces single-page sessions, so following the product's advice would have permanently suppressed diagnosis of that step. Pages-per-session and bounce are now read as corroboration only. Separating a conversion problem from a traffic problem is a real doctor move and it is deferred until there is a sourced threshold, not approximated with a plausible one.

Risk

Level: 2 — backend-only and contract-preserving. Unit-tested at the level that matters (selection, exclusion, abstention, headline copy, and the bookable-card interaction); not exercised against production data.

Risks identified

  • Owner-visible copy changes even though the contract does not. When the gait abstains, the headline sentence differs from what an owner saw last month, and in some months no step will be named at all. Mitigation: every abstention sentence is descriptive and explains what was observed; this is the honest outcome, but Dave should expect it and not read a null leakStageKey as a bug.
  • The gait can abstain more often than expected on thin accounts. That is the intent — a diagnosis on 6 visitors was never real. Mitigation: the reasoning is in the logs, so frequency is measurable before anyone tunes a threshold.
  • Thresholds are judgment, not evidence. Mitigation: there is now one, it reuses the project's existing floor, and its comment says it is operational rather than a benchmark. The two invented ones were removed rather than re-argued.
  • Exclusions redirect the verdict, and that is the dangerous direction. Corroboration only strengthens or withholds confidence, but an exclusion changes which step gets named — which is exactly how the inverted verdict happened. Mitigation: the global-minimum rule means an exclusion can only ever produce an abstention, never a different named step.
  • The remaining organic signals are site-level. top10QueryCount counts queries, not per-page performance. Mitigation: it is used only to withhold a click-through diagnosis, never to assert one, and the abstain copy claims only what the count supports.

Blast radius: buildConversions and buildHeadline are called from exactly one place, PatientJourneyService.assemblePatientJourney. buildConversions's return type gains a field (additive; the destructuring caller is updated). extractMetrics is consumed only by aggregateMetrics; KeyDataResult gains two optional-to-ignore fields that no current frontend consumer reads.

Pushback

  • The gait keeps min-rate as its final tie-break, which looks like it changed nothing. It is worth being honest about this: on a multiplicative funnel, lowest rate genuinely is highest improvement-per-point, so the old rule was not wrong about the tie-break — it was wrong about answering at all without gating on sample size, baseline, and corroboration, and about never abstaining. Replacing the ranking metric on a hunch would have been the re-derivation trap. The gates are the change.
  • The reasoning is logged, not returned. That keeps this Level 2 but means the owner still cannot see why. That is the whole point of brick 2, and brick 1 is worth little if brick 2 never lands.

What was verified, and what was not

Verified — with a receipt

  • The naive selector and the untouched Clarity extractor were read on origin/dev/dave after a fetch, not on a local branch.
  • Both typechecks exit clean (backend and frontend). 70 tests pass across the three affected suites. npm run check:conventions reports zero structural violations.
  • An independent cross-model adversary reviewed this diff and returned FIX-FIRST. It proved the inverted verdict with a passing test against the real code, caught the GSC position-0 sentinel being read as a first-place ranking, and refuted two of the three thresholds. All must-fix and should-fix findings are applied above. That review was the point: the same model wrote this code and its tests, and the first draft's test suite asserted the central defect as correct.

Not verified — stated, not hidden

  • Split out of this PR: the Clarity extraction that was originally bundled here now lives in plans/07222026-clarity-cro-signals, held as a draft. It could not be proven — three of four sampled Clarity projects capture no sessions at all — and this PR should not carry a piece nobody can show working.
  • The gait has not been run against a production account. Nobody has yet seen how often it abstains on real data — that is the first thing the logs will answer after it lands.
  • No claim is made that the gait's verdicts are correct. It is better-gated than what it replaces, and one class of wrong verdict has been removed and pinned by a test; whether its diagnoses hold up is exactly what the follow-on below exists to answer.

Follow-on: the exam gate (named here so it is not lost — do not build it in this brick)

the-engine.md is blunt about this: a diagnosis engine that is never scored against known outcomes is a plausible-but-wrong pattern waiting to happen, and the exam is "the part that matters most and doesn't exist yet."

The question this brick cannot answer about itself: when the gait names a binding constraint and that constraint is treated, does the number actually move? Answering it requires, per the plan's own terms:

  • Out-of-sample validity — no pattern drives a verdict until it holds on a different account and time than it was found on.
  • Prospective, blind-locked cases — a dated, locked diagnosis on each account before the outcome is known. Certification begins with prospective, not retrospective.
  • Anti-contamination — the treatment's author cannot also be its judge. The plan names this its sharpest trap, and the session that produced this brick has direct evidence for it: a same-author judge returned 7-0-1 for a change that a neutral cross-model judge reversed to 0-2-6.

The cheap first step, when someone picks this up: the gait already logs a structured, dated diagnosis on every assembly. That log is the raw material for a prospective answer key — it exists from the moment this lands, and costs nothing extra to start accumulating.

Landing gate — this is not done until it runs unattended

Code written, tests green and typecheck clean is not the finish line. A spec that dies in a chat is a failure, not a deliverable. This brick is done when all of the following are true, in order:

  1. PR opened against dev/dave from claude/diagnostic-gait-brick1.
  2. Dave reviews and merges it.
  3. Deployed to dev; GET /api/patient-journey returns a real account's funnel with the payload shape unchanged.
  4. The [patient-journey] assembled funnel log line shows the per-step reasoning on a real account — diagnosisBasis, excludedBy, corroboration populated.
  5. It runs unattended across a full assembly cycle with no error.
  6. The abstention rate on real accounts is confirmed acceptable — not merely observed. A coordinator that abstains on most accounts is a dead feature, not a safe one. If abstentions are dominated by insufficient-sample or expected-for-position on accounts a human would happily diagnose, the gates are mis-set and this brick needs revision before brick 2 builds on it.

Until step 5, the status pill above stays In Progress. Anyone updating it to Completed without step 5 is reporting a story instead of a result.
