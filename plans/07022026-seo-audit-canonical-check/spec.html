Why

The AI SEO Audit app (/admin/apps) scored the exact page that started this whole investigation — One Endodontics' Fredericksburg location, whose canonical pointed at a dead legacy URL — 8/8 on "Canonical tag" and 30/30 on the whole "Page Title & Canonical" card (owner screenshot, 2026-07-02). The check only verifies that a canonical exists and whether it's absolute vs relative; it never checks that the canonical actually names the page it sits on, or the site's own domain. That blind spot let 71 wrong canonicals across three live client sites (including Garrison posts canonicalizing to an internal *.sites.getalloro.com hostname) hide behind perfect scores. An audit that can't see the defect it exists to catch quietly certifies broken data.

What

The canonical criterion becomes a three-part correctness check: (1) tag present, (2) same host as the audited site's real domain (relative counts as same-host), (3) path matches the audited page's actual serving path after normalization (trailing slash, case). Full credit requires all three; a present-but-wrong canonical scores zero for the criterion and the finding names the mismatch (expected /locations/fredericksburg-office, found /fredericksburg-office). Done when re-auditing a page with a deliberately wrong canonical fails the criterion with that message, and a correct page still passes at full weight.

Context

  • src/services/ai-seo-audit/ — the audit engine (Rev 9 scoring contract: rubric, full-weight pass, importance-weighted pages). Canonical-related logic appears in scoringEngine.ts, scoringShared.ts, and sourceReadinessScoring.ts; first execution step is locating the exact criterion implementation for the "Page Title & Canonical" card before changing anything.
  • The audited page's expected path/host are derivable from the audit target's own URL (urlCollectorService.ts / auditTargetExecutionService.ts collect and fetch the URLs) — the comparison needs no new data source.
  • Frontend (SeoAuditApp surfaces) renders whatever criteria/insight the backend returns — expected to be backend-only; verify no frontend contract change is needed.

Reference analog: the neighboring criteria in the same scoring card (title-length, robots) — match their result shape, weighting, and finding-text style exactly.

Risk

Level: 2 — scores will drop on sites with wrong canonicals, which is the point but is owner-visible.

  • Level 2 Historical audit runs scored under the old rubric; new runs will dip for affected sites and the two numbers aren't comparable. Mitigation: note the rubric change in the changelog; the 4 recently fixed sites will pass anyway.
  • Level 1 Legitimate cross-page canonicals (a duplicate page deliberately canonicalizing elsewhere on the same site) would now flag. Mitigation: same-host + different-path scores partial credit with an informational finding rather than zero — deliberate consolidation is valid SEO; cross-host is the hard failure.

Blast radius: AI SEO Audit scoring for every org that runs it; no data writes, no generation-pipeline overlap.
